# Semester Project Outline: The Ghost in the Machine

## Title: Algorithmic Rationality vs. Social Legitimacy in AI-Driven Public Health Governance

## 1. Introduction (2-3 pages)
*   **The AI Pivot**: Contextualizing the shift from human-mediated to algorithm-mediated public health interventions.
*   **Research Question**: How does the tension between "algorithmic efficiency" and "social legitimacy" reshape the social-economic contract in public health?
*   **Significance**: Bridging the gap between technical optimization and institutional acceptance.

### 1.5 Core Research Questions
1.  **Technical vs. Social Legitimacy**: How does algorithmic efficiency (output legitimacy) conflict with human-centered social legitimacy (process legitimacy) in high-stakes health decisions?
2.  **The Optimality Trap**: What are the trade-offs between "optimal" resource allocation and "social acceptability" in autonomous distribution models?
3.  **Algorithmic Re-embedding**: How can AI agents be "re-embedded" into existing social norms and institutional trust structures, following Polanyi's framework for the digital age?
4.  **The Rationality-Trust Paradox**: To what extent does "algorithmic rationality" create a "trust deficit" that inadvertently undermines the public health outcomes it seeks to optimize?

## 2. Core Theoretical Frameworks (Comparison Component)
*   **Theory A: Rational Choice Theory (RCT) / Neoclassical Political Economy**
    *   AI as the "Hyper-Rational" actor.
    *   Focus on Output Legitimacy (Allocative Efficiency, Information Asymmetry).
*   **Theory B: Social Embeddedness (Polanyi) / New Institutionalism**
    *   The economy (and AI) embedded in social norms.
    *   Focus on Process Legitimacy (Trust, Equity, Accountability).
*   **Synthesis/Tension**: The "Double Movement" of AI - technical liberation vs. social backlash.

## 3. Analytical Dimensions (Interaction of Social & Economic Processes)
*   **The Efficiency-Equity Trade-off**: When optimal resource allocation (Economic) conflicts with social perceptions of fairness (Social).
*   **The Trust Deficit**: Can a "black box" algorithm generate the necessary social capital for public compliance?
*   **Epistemology of Modeling**: How the assumptions of AI models (e.g., utilitarianism) differ from the lived social reality of health outcomes.

## 4. Applied Case Study: Resource Allocation & Vaccine Distribution
*   Comparing a RCT-driven allocation model (Efficiency focus) with a Socially-mediated model (Trust/Equity focus).

## 5. Critical Discussion: The Future of Governance
*   Explainable AI (XAI) as a bridge for social legitimacy.
*   The role of the state in re-embedding algorithms into the social fabric.

## 6. Conclusion
*   Final assessment of the theoretical tension.
*   Implications for future health policy.
